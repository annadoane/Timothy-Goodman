Timothy Goodman's style is characterized by a playful yet poignant blend of illustration and typography, often infused with personal narratives and social commentary. His work combines bold colors and hand-drawn elements, creating a distinctive visual language that resonates with a wide audience. Influenced by his experiences in New York City and the diverse artistic movements surrounding him, Goodman often addresses themes of identity, connection, and the human experience. His ability to merge graphic design with storytelling allows him to engage viewers on both an emotional and intellectual level, making his art both accessible and thought-provoking.

Timothy Goodman draws significant inspiration from Keith Haring, whose vibrant and energetic style resonates deeply with Goodman’s own artistic approach. Haring's emphasis on social issues and community engagement mirrors Goodman’s commitment to addressing contemporary themes through art. The bold lines, playful figures, and impactful messaging that define Haring's work serve as a backdrop for Goodman, encouraging him to explore the intersection of art and activism. This influence is evident in Goodman’s own murals and public installations, where he seeks to foster connection and provoke thought, much like Haring did during his time. By channeling Haring's spirit, Goodman continues to push boundaries in his art, aiming to inspire dialogue and reflection in the viewer.
